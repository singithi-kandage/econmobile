  • Tips for Writing Practical Tests

    Posted Sep 20, 2017 11:24 AM

    Hi everyone,

    With the first test coming up, I thought it would be helpful to post some tips regarding preparing for tests with practical components.

    Planning is key! Although you will feel pressure to "get started", reviewing the entire requirement and planning your approach will make things much easier in the long run. Quality of work is most important. 

    Use your time wisely. You may find yourself waiting for the system to complete something (e.g. OS installation, role installation, etc.) Can you think of a more perfect time to put your screenshot labels in your document? By reading through the requirements, you know, in most cases, exactly what your screenshots are going to be. If you create the labels ahead of time, assembling your document becomes much much easier. Plus there is a much better chance of not missing something.

    Build your document as you go. I've seen many students in the past just taking screenshots and trying to sort them out after all your technical work is finished, That often leads to a time crunch. A well done partially finished test is far better than a bunch of screenshots dropped into a document, hoping they are in the right order.

    And finally, again dealing with time management, you are allowed a fixed amount of time to complete the work. I always provide warnings of time remaining. If you decide to leave assembling your document to the end, you should stop all technical work at the 15 minute warning. That will barely give you enough time to get your document together. If you build your document as you go, you should stop all technical work at the 5 minute warning and take a few minutes to review your document before submitting. You should not even think about making changes to your document at the 2 minute warning. Immediately upload and submit. You have until the beginning of the minute of the time due, not the end (i.e. if the test ends at 9:00, the submission must be completed by 9:00:00, not 9:00:59.

    Hope this helps you prepare for a successful test.

    Jim
